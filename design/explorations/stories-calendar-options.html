A · Editorial story grid Three story cards: one hero (60% width) plus two supporting. Each with image, category, headline, excerpt, date. Magazine-spread feel.
Why thisMost editorial / brand-feeling. Lets the academy tell stories — about sailors, results, programs. Builds personality across visits as content rotates.
RiskDemands a content engine. If three stories sit dated for two months, the section makes the academy look quiet. Needs ongoing editorial commitment.

D · Mixed stream feed One vertical feed. Stories + events + race results all in chronological order, with category pills (Story / Event / Result / News). Reads like a coach's running diary or social feed.
Why thisMost "alive" feeling — section reads as a continuous activity stream. Easiest to populate (anything you publish goes in the feed). Pills create instant scannability.
RiskLess editorial weight — no single story "stars." Risks reading as social-media-on-website, which can feel low-effort if not curated.

Pick a treatment. A if the academy will commit to ongoing editorial. B if conversion (registrations) is the priority. C if you want both. D if you want the section to feel like a living stream with low content-per-update overhead.
