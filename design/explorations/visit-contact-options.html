A · Single big CTA Full-bleed photo. One headline. One short proof line. Two buttons (primary: Schedule a visit / secondary: View programs). The most confident close.
Why thisCloses the page on a single, focused action. Most cinematic. Pairs with the hero — same visual register, bookend feeling.
RiskOnly serves one visitor type at a time. Visitors who don't want to "schedule a visit" right now bounce off without a path.

B · Multi-path doors Four doors framed as visitor questions. Each leads to the right deeper page. Most useful when visitors have wildly different intents — which is true here (parents, adult learners, racers, press).
Why thisRoutes every visitor type to the right next step. Acts as a self-service triage layer. Flexible — doors can rotate (e.g., add "I want to coach" door during instructor recruitment).
RiskLess brand-dramatic than a single-CTA close. Reads more like a sitemap than a moment.

C · Inline inquiry form Form on the left ("Tell us about your sailor" — name, age, experience, interest), contact info panel on the right (phone, email, address). Most actionable, most conversion-optimized.
Why thisRemoves friction — visitors take action without leaving the page. Form fields qualify the lead automatically (age, experience, interest). Coach can route the inquiry without back-and-forth.
RiskMost "lead-gen funnel" energy — can feel transactional next to the rest of the page. Needs a real backend (form submission, routing, response SLA).
