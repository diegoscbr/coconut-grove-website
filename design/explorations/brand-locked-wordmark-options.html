Wordmark lockup · pick a layout

All three use the locked system. The difference: how the pennant + "Instructional Center · Coconut Grove · 1946" are arranged in the site header. This is the lockup that lives in the nav of every page.

INSTRUCTIONAL CENTER · COCONUT GROVE · 1946
A · Single deckline Pennant + one horizontal line of letterspaced caps. Most minimal. Reads like a stadium plate or a yacht-club deck inscription. Best for slim, fixed-position headers. Scales narrow on mobile (truncate to "INSTRUCTIONAL CENTER").
Instructional Center
Coconut Grove · 1946
B · Stacked two-line Pennant left, name in display weight on top, location and year as a small kicker below. Most legible. Closest to how Sperry, Patagonia, J. Crew lock up brand+kicker. Best general-purpose lockup. Works at any size, mobile-friendly without truncation.
Instructional Center
Coconut Grove · 1946
C · Centered institutional Pennant on top, two centered lines below. Most formal — reads like a crest. Closer to a museum or a club masthead than a modern brand header. Risk: takes more vertical space. Best as a footer mark or splash-page lockup, less so a sticky header.

Pick a wordmark lockup. Recommended default: B · Stacked two-line — most usable across contexts, doesn't strain at small sizes, and the kicker reads naturally as a sub-mark. After this, next decision is the Programs grid card design (section 2 of the page).
