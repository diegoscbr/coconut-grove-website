Homepage section structure · what comes after the hero

Hero is locked. Now the spine of the page: what sections appear, in what order. The order itself is a thesis — the second section is what you most want a first-time visitor to understand. Four structures below, each making a different bet on what that is.

Locked so far Headline THE PREMIER / SAILING ACADEMY / IN BISCAYNE BAY · Since 1946 · Accessible · World-Class
Subhead For 80 years, the Bay's training ground for sailors at every level. Now, taking it to the next — international wins and Olympic podiums.
Highlighted row = the spine — the section that defines the page's center of gravity.
A · Programs-as-spine
Mouratoglou mirror
  1. Hero headline + subhead + visual
  2. Programs grid 5–6 pillar cards (Junior Academy, Adult Sailing, Race Team, High Performance, Camps, Coaching Education)
  3. The Pathway visual ladder, first sail → Olympics
  4. The Coaching Maru-led, staff, methodology
  5. Heritage + Champions 1946 anchor, alumni results
  6. The Bay territory advantage
  7. Stories / Calendar news + upcoming events
  8. Visit / Contact
Why this Mouratoglou's exact spine: programs first because the visitor's first question is "what do you offer?" Pathway in second position turns the grid into a story — these aren't separate boxes, they're rungs. Safest, most familiar shape.
B · Pathway-as-spine
The ladder is the page
  1. Hero
  2. The Pathway full-bleed visual ladder, first sail → Olympic podium, with rungs labeled by age/level
  3. Programs grid cards drilling into each rung
  4. The Coaching
  5. Heritage + Champions
  6. The Bay
  7. Stories / Calendar
  8. Visit / Contact
Why this Bets that CGSC IC's most distinctive claim is the through-line. Visitor sees a single ladder before any program details — turns "we offer juniors and adults" into "we move sailors from here to here." Most aspirational. Risk: a parent looking for a Saturday class has to scroll past it.
C · Coaching-as-spine
The Maru era, foregrounded
  1. Hero
  2. The Coaching Maru + staff bios, methodology, "what world-class coaching looks like"
  3. The Pathway
  4. Programs grid
  5. Heritage + Champions
  6. The Bay
  7. Stories / Calendar
  8. Visit / Contact
Why this Bets that the new chapter is the coaching upgrade — and that visitors will judge "premier" by the people, not the brochure. Mouratoglou himself was the spine of his own academy's site for years. Risk: makes the page feel personality-driven; if Maru leaves, the site needs a rewrite.
D · Heritage-as-spine
Earn the ambition before claiming it
  1. Hero
  2. Heritage moment 1946 → today: photo essay, anchor numbers (alumni, Olympians, decades), then the pivot to "now"
  3. The Pathway
  4. Programs grid
  5. The Coaching
  6. The Bay
  7. Stories / Calendar
  8. Visit / Contact
Why this Bets that the strongest move against "we just claimed to be premier" is to walk visitors through 80 years of receipts before showing them the program. Most credible / least-aspirational-feeling. Closer to a museum or institution's site than a tennis academy's. Risk: backwards-looking energy in section 2.

Pick the spine — section 2 is the bet. Order of sections 3+ is fungible. If you want to combine (e.g., "B's pathway second + C's coaching third"), say so.
