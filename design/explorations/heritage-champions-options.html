C · Magazine opener + champions strip
Tell one founding story, then the strip of receipts
Since 1946
Three sailors, one rented dock, a borrowed Snipe — and 80 years later, an academy.
Bob Henley, Carl Webb, and Frances Tomlinson founded the Coconut Grove Sailing Club in the spring of 1946 with one borrowed boat and a stretch of the Bay no one else was using. Within five years they had 40 members; within twenty, the Bay's first all-women race team. Eighty years later, the academy that grew from that dock has trained six Olympians, three world champions, and roughly fifteen thousand kids who just learned to love sailing.
