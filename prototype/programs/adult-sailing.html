Adult Sailing

The parallel adult track.

CGSC Adult Sailing is for adults who want to actually sail — not race, not train for an Olympic campaign, just get on the water consistently with people who know what they're doing. Weekly group sessions, mixed-level boats, real Bay conditions.

Whether you've never touched a tiller or you sailed as a teenager and want back in, the same program works. We pair you with the right boat and the right group. Gear, insurance, and boats are included.

The Bay

Year-round, on a body of water you can read.

Biscayne Bay gives us 365 sailable days, water that averages 76°F, and consistent afternoon thermals. The same conditions year over year — which is what makes it an honest place to learn.

Our dock is a five-minute walk from downtown Coconut Grove. You won't drive to a marina on the other side of town; you'll bike or walk from the neighborhood.

FAQ

Common questions.

I've never sailed. Can I really start here?
Yes. About half the adult group starts with zero experience. We have boats and rigging for absolute beginners; you'll be on the water on day one with a coach in the boat or in a chase RIB nearby.
What's the equipment situation?
Boats, sails, PFDs, and instruction are all included. Bring sun protection, water, and shoes that can get wet. Anything else, we have on the dock.
How does this differ from a community sailing class elsewhere?
The coaches. Our adult sessions are taught by the same staff that runs the Race Team and the High Performance Center. You get the depth of a competitive program at the pace of a recreational one.
What if I want to start racing later?
You can. About a quarter of our adult sailors end up on the racing track — most join the Friday-night beer-can series first. The Race Team coaches will see you sail every week and tell you when you're ready.
